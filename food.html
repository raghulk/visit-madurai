Food of Madurai

Mutton kari dosa at Simmakkal Konar

No dish reveals Madurai’s strong affinity for mutton like the kari (mutton) dosa at this local institution. The Konars are one of Madurai’s very own cow and goatherds, offal dishes are a big part of their menu. This triple decker treat is built on a thick dosa, with an omelette and a layer of minced mutton.

Murugan Idli Shop

This idli eatery is now on every foodie’s wish list when they visit Chennai. But the Murugan Idli story began in Madurai just a few streets away from the Madurai Meenakshi temple. Murugan’s idlis are fluffy and border on sticky, and are served with four trademark chutneys and their signature idli powder. While idlis are their calling card, regulars also swear by Murugan’s ghee soaked onion uttappam and sweet pongal.

Kola Unrundai at Chandran Mess

This is the quintessential Madurai family restaurant where the kitchen is still supervised on a daily basis by the owner and his family members. Packaged masalas are a taboo here; the kitchen still grinds masalas in old school stone pestles. That’s one reason why dishes like the kola urundai burst with flavours and melt in the mouth.
